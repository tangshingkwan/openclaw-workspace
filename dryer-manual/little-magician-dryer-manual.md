## ⚠️ Important Safety Precautions Before Use

### I. Electrical Safety

1. Plug the power cord into a dedicated single-phase three-pin 220V AC power outlet with 10A or above. The grounding wire of the outlet must be reliably grounded. A leakage protector is recommended.
2. Do not place flammable or volatile substances (such as gasoline, alcohol) near the dryer during operation to avoid fire hazards.
3. Do not install the dryer in bathrooms or other damp places where it may be exposed to rain, to prevent electric shock or leakage.
4. The power plug should be easily accessible during normal use. Always unplug when there is a power outage, moving, not in use, or before cleaning. Do not plug or unplug with wet hands.
5. Do not excessively bend, stretch, twist, bundle, or place heavy objects on the power cord. If the power cord or plug is damaged, it must be replaced by a repair technician to avoid danger.
6. Do not place the dryer on packaging foam for operation, as this may cause fire hazards due to poor heat dissipation or other external factors.
7. When cleaning the drum interior or dryer exterior, wipe with a soft damp cloth only. Do not rinse with water directly, and do not use volatile oils (such as alcohol, gasoline) for wiping.
8. If using chemical cleaning products on clothes, do not use the drum dryer to dry them.
9. Lint must not accumulate around the drum dryer.
10. Adequate ventilation must be provided to avoid gases flowing back from the appliance igniting other substances, including open flames.

### II. Usage Requirements

1. Clothes must be thoroughly spin-dried before placing in the dryer. (Clothes wrung out by hand still contain large amounts of water, which may leak and increase drying time — avoid this.)
2. Before drying, carefully check clothes and remove lighters, matches, coins, hairpins, paper clips, keys, and other sharp metal objects from pockets.
3. Fasten zippers and buttons, and turn clothes inside out before drying. (This reduces collision sounds with the drum.)
4. Do not dry clothes with care labels stating "do not wash with water" or "dry clean only — do not use dryer."
5. Do not dry woolen, cashmere, leather, or fur items in the dryer — leather and fur will be damaged, wool will shrink and deform.
6. After each use, lint on the intake air filter and exhaust air filter cotton must be cleaned.
7. It is best to dry clothes of the same or similar fabric type together.
8. Do not dry clothes with the following requirements: Do not tumble dry, hang to dry, drip dry, flat dry, or air dry.

> ⚠️ **Note:** This product is a household drum dryer, not for commercial use. For commercial use, please purchase a dedicated commercial dryer from our company. This dryer should be used under supervision.

## Installation

The dryer should be placed on a level, sturdy floor, table, or dedicated stand. If used in a narrow space, an exhaust fan can be installed to vent moist air outdoors.

### 1. Level Positioning
Do not invert the dryer. When placed on a level floor or table, ensure the surface is sturdy, level, and not tilted.

### 2. Optional Installation
**Dryer Stand Installation**
When installing the dryer on a stand, the stand must be sturdy and reliable. If the stand is floor-mounted, it must be stable with a firm connection between the stand and the wall to ensure stability. (Stand is a non-standard accessory.)

## Cleaning & Maintenance

### Dryer Body Cleaning
Unplug the power cord before cleaning. Wipe with a soft damp cloth. For stubborn dirt, use kitchen cleaner, then wipe dry with a clean cloth.

> ⚠️ **Caution:** Do not use thinners, gasoline, or alcohol to wipe the body.

### Filter Cleaning

> ⚠️ **Important:** Filters must be installed before operating the dryer. Clean after every use. Unplug before cleaning.

**1. Removing the filter:**
Insert fingers into the two holes beside the filter cover center, pull upward.

**2. Cleaning lint:**
Open the filter, clean the filter screen with a brush.

**3. Reinstalling filter:**
Close the filter, align with filter installation opening and insert.

Order: Lower filter → Upper filter → Front filter cover

### Interior Cleaning
It is recommended to clean the dryer interior every 20-25 uses.
1. Open the door, remove the filter, insert a vacuum cleaner into the filter installation openings to suck out dust and lint.

| (☁️) Cotton/Linen | For cotton and linen |

**Other buttons:**
- (℃) Temperature control
- (🔒) Child lock indicator
- (⚙️) UV light on/off
- (⏯) Start/Pause
- (⏻) Power on/off

### Operating Instructions

1. Press **(⏻)** to turn power on/off.
2. **Select a program** — Press the corresponding button. The indicator light flashes when selected. For Timer, press repeatedly to cycle through: 0:15 → 0:30 → 1:00 → 1:30 → 2:00 → 2:30.
3. **Temperature selection** — When using Timer program, temperature can be set to 60°C, 50°C, 40°C, or 0°C (no heat). Press **(℃)** to switch.
4. Press **(⏯)** to start. The dryer begins after 2 seconds. Press again to pause (can add/remove clothes — be careful of hot steam!). Press again to resume. If started directly without selecting a program, it runs in Smart mode.
5. **Child lock** — When running, press and hold **(℃) + (⚙️)** for 2 seconds. The lock icon 🔒 lights up and buttons are disabled. Repeat to unlock.
6. **Auto-tumble** — If door is not opened after drying ends, the dryer runs 1 minute every 30 minutes (up to 36 hours) to tumble clothes and prevent wrinkles.
7. **Shoe drying** — When using Shoe program, the drum does not rotate; only hot air circulates.
8. **UV light** — Press **(⚙️)** to enable/disable UV lamp. When enabled, the UV indicator flashes.

## Troubleshooting

| Problem | Possible Cause | Solution |
|---------|----------------|----------|
| **Dryer won't start** | Power outage or tripped breaker | Wait for power to restore |
| | Loose power connection | Check and secure power connection |
| | Overloaded with wet clothes | Max dry weight: 5.0kg (wet weight) |
| | Temperature controller tripped | Call technician to replace |
| **Dryer is hot externally / smells hot** | Lint filter clogged with debris | Clean lint filter before and after each use |
| | Exhaust vent or duct blocked | Clear blockages |
| **Clothes not drying** | Too full | Dry in smaller batches |
| | Filters clogged, affecting airflow | Clean intake/exhaust filters regularly |
| | Clothes tangled | Shake out clothes before re-drying |
| | Machine in cramped, poorly ventilated space | Reposition machine |
| **Excessive wrinkles** | Too full | Do not overfill |
| | Not removed promptly after cycle | Remove clothes immediately |
| | Clothes not properly sorted | Sort by fabric type before drying |
| | Temperature too high | Follow care label instructions |
| **Clothes shrinking** | Over-dried | Follow care label instructions. Check frequently for shrink-prone items. Remove while slightly damp and hang or lay flat to finish drying |
| **Pilling on clothes** | Normal friction with synthetic and permanent-press fabrics | Use fabric softener. Spray starch on collars/cuffs when ironing. Turn clothes inside out to reduce friction |
